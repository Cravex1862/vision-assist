# Vision Assist

Vision Assist is a mobile and web application designed to help blind and visually impaired individuals navigate by pointing their phone camera and tapping anywhere on the screen to describe what is going on. Users can also tap and hold the screen to ask specific questions.

Live Demo: https://vision.ashwinchoudhury.me
Repository: https://github.com/Cravex1862/vision-assist

---

## Features

- **Single Tap to Describe:** Tap anywhere on the screen to capture a frame from the camera and receive a clear description of what is in front of you.
- **Tap and Hold to Ask:** Press and hold the screen to use your microphone and ask specific questions about your environment (such as asking for obstacles, identifying colors, or reading text).
- **Accessibility First Design:** Zero cluttered buttons or complex user interfaces. The entire screen acts as a giant touchpad, optimized for screen readers and gesture-based navigation.

---

## How to Use

1. **Grant Permissions:** Allow the app to access your camera and microphone when prompted.
2. **Point:** Aim your phone's camera at your environment.
3. **Tap:** Tap anywhere on the screen. The app takes a snapshot and narrates what it sees.
4. **Hold and Speak:** Long-press anywhere on the screen, ask a question, and release. The app will capture the scene and answer your exact query.
